MANDATORY GROUNDING RULE

Every factual claim, critique, or recommendation MUST be grounded in at least one of:

a Digital Twin portal URL you opened via navigation, or

a repo-relative path explicitly indexed for inspection (e.g., via docs/INSPECTION_INDEX.md), or

a Digital Twin mirror that includes a link back to an authoritative eudr-dmi-gil source

If grounding is not possible, label it explicitly as:

Assumption, or

Evidence gap

Ungrounded items must never be presented as facts.

Evidence gap identification
Identify evidence gaps revealed by the example AOI report.
Distinguish between:

missing artefacts,
unclear acceptance criteria,
unverifiable assumptions.
Classify each gap as suitable for DAO (Stakeholders) review or
DAO (Developers) action.

DAO proposals (Stakeholders)
Convert observations from the example AOI report into DAO (Stakeholders)
questions and proposals.

Focus on:

transparency and inspectability,
evidence sufficiency,
clarity of requirements and acceptance criteria.
Do not propose implementation or code-level changes in this mode.

DAO proposals (Developers)
Propose concrete DAO (Developers) change requests based on the example
AOI report.

Each proposal MUST specify:

affected files or artefacts,
deterministic output requirements,
validation or test criteria,
regeneration guarantees.
Do not make compliance or certification claims.

Boundary and limitation clarification
Explain which conclusions cannot be drawn from the example AOI report.

Explicitly distinguish between:

inspection vs certification,
example artefacts vs production evidence,
grounded findings vs assumptions.
This clarification is mandatory whenever there is a risk of regulatory
overreach or misinterpretation.
